Portfolio Augusto Gandia

(November 2025 - Mendoza, Argentina)

The investigation addresses the projected doubling of residential buildings globally by 2050. This crisis necessitates the reorientation of the organic legacy from fuel to dynamic shells. The thesis posits that "hanging elasticos"—bridges between our minds and the possibilities of matter—must be instantiated in this series of interdisciplinary habitable spaces, inviting the exploration of a novel carbon ontology for reconnecting architecture theory and practice.

Gradual Assemblies (ETH Zurich, 2018)


Gradual Assemblies serves as the overarching urbanistic proposal, demonstrating a new paradigm for metropolitan growth and density. Rejecting conventional top-down master planning, this project proposes a continuous, evolving, and resilient urban infrastructure. It champions the idea of Synergetic Infrastructure—a system where decentralised components (like the subsequent house modules) organically aggregate over time, optimising energy and social throughput through self-regulating geometry. This approach directly confronts global urban problems by providing a scalable, non-finite model for growth that remains perpetually adaptable to changing human needs and environmental pressures.

Fulcrum (MIT, 2023)


The Fulcrum House is the standardised, high-efficiency, modular component within the Gradual Assemblies urban system. Designed using principles of innovative geometry, the module maximises usable space while minimising material usage, embodying Fuller's pursuit of ephemeralization in shelter. It functions as a resilient core unit, engineered for rapid fabrication and deployment. Its structural integrity and standardised connection points allow it to serve as a foundational element—a true "fulcrum"—around which larger, more complex social and functional clusters can form within the Gradual Assemblies framework, ensuring universal access to quality housing.

Stealth House (MIT, 2020-)


The Stealth House represents the critical final step in the design science pipeline: the implementation of an alloplastically adapted module. Where the Fulcrum House is the efficient core, the Stealth House demonstrates how that core is environmentally and socially customised. Alloplasty, in this context, refers to altering the external environment or the module’s boundary to achieve fitness (as opposed to autoplasty, where the system changes itself internally). The Stealth House uses active, responsive components—such as dynamic facades, variable shading, and integrated micro-energy harvesting—to "adapt" to its specific location, maximising thermal performance and minimising energy waste. This ensures the module is not a static object, but an active participant in the Regenerative Design of its immediate ecosystem.
