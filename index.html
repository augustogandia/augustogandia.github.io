Hypo-skins: A Carbon Ontology for the Housing Crisis

Today, human-made mass already exceeds biomass and Bizzantine assemblies consume some 40% of total energy production. The organic legacy emerges as a unique opportunity for reorienting its primary use from burning primarily for motion to assembling shells for embracing our dynamic realities. This investigation proposes a carbon ontology that addresses the projected doubling of buildings globally by 2050.

Hypo-skins are portals to bridge the inhabitant, the site, and high-performance carbon materials across space and time. As in the tale of the Mont Blanc ascent (to be updated), this series of interdisciplinary instances aims to unveil an overlooked contribution, a non-hierarchical, relational, and dynamic diagram (tbu) that reconnects architecture theory and practice.

Gradual Assemblies (ETH Zurich, 2020)


This design studio is part of the MAS ETH DFAB and explores , a student from the Media Lab explores m(a)cro scale light artifacts. They start as a series of generic plywood and plexiglass Light Clouds, and evolve into site and time-sensitive Light Clouds. The design process starts by experiencing different sites while searching for interesting moments of light and history (e.g. the carpenter center of visual arts). These are recorded through computer vision as image schemata of emerging light phenomena, later materialized as inhabitable bays or islands of transparent stacked intensities.

Cybermodelling/Demercan ()


This project builds upon a workshop titled Topographies of Light, conducted at MIT in collaboration with Aileen Iverson () and Andy Payne (McNeel). While we first explored differential habitats through deductive reasoning, this subsequent exercise consisted of setting constraints by which the form could find itself. As such, we designed a series of hydrophilic elastic shells through the internal forces of the material and other essential elements, such as gravity, sound, and light. Each participant assembled a portal for linking a sensing skin (physical interface) with its digital counterpart, enabling a "sur le motif" multiplicity of possibilities beyond gestural instinctive approaches. fuse roof and wall, surface and structure, openings and background, unlocking families of antimodernist spaces inhabited beyond projective experiences.

Assamblage: Aileen Iverson, Augusto Gandia, Richa Gupta, Andy Payne
