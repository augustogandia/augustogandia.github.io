Immergent Manifolds: A Carbon Architecture for the Housing Crisis

Immergent (immanent + emergence) Manifolds (portals for connecting the inhabitant, environmentally benign carbon processing and spacetime across scales) are initially explored as a novel design methodology that helps us modulate the striated and smooth space of elegant streamlined habitats. As in the old Mont Blanc tale, a series of interdisciplinary instances unveil the alternative path necessary to successfully traverse such a sinuous relational, no-hierarchical, and dynamic risky ascent, addressing Balmat's and the scientific elite's attempt to erase the expedition's initiator (*add emails).

Topographies of Light (MIT IAP, 2023)


This teaching and research project explores m(a)cro scale artifacts. Dexter, an student from the Media Lab proposed a series of plywood and plexiglass light sculptures Light Clouds. These generic explorations evolved into context and time-sensitive Light Sculptures. Their design process starts by experiencing different spaces while searching for interesting moments with light and history. The selected candidates are recorded through computer vision as revisitable image schemata, materialized as bespoke plexiglass stacks of inhabitable shells.

Assamblage: Callender, Gandia, Iverson-Radtke

Carbon House (ARPA-E, 2020-2024)


Building upon the Fulcrum House study on design parameters and benign materials, this research project extends the scope to dis/assamblable buildings with freely oriented components and expressive details

Assamblage: Goulthorpe, Gandia, Chatzinikolis, dECOI Architects

Cinematic Details (Carbon House, 2022)


This investigation explores a design method for composite details. Through interdisciplinary work with a product designer, integral designs are developed beyond performance and optimization.

Assamblage: Guerin, Gandia, Goulthorpe, dECOI Architects
