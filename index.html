Immergent Manifolds: A Carbon Ontology for the Housing Crisis

Immergent (immanent + emergence) Manifolds (portals for connecting the inhabitant, the site, and high-performing environmentally-benign carbon materials across space and time) are a novel design methodology for exploring elegant streamlined high-performing composite habitats. As in the Mont Blanc first ascent (*add emails), a series of interdisciplinary instances unveil the story modified by Balmat's and the scientific elite to erase the initiator. Beyond performance, it proposes an alternative non-hierarchical, relational, and dynamic path for achieving such a endeavors.

Topographies of Light (MIT IAP, 2023)


As part of this teaching and research project, a student from the Media Lab explores m(a)cro scale light artifacts. They start as a series of generic plywood and plexiglass Light Clouds, and evolve into site and time-sensitive Light Sculptures. First, different sites are experienced while searching for interesting moments of light and history (e.g. the carpenter center of visual arts). These are recorded throughout the day by using computer vision, resulting in image schematas of emerging light phenomena, that is later materialized as laser cut transparent stacks and islands, forms of inhabitable shells.

Assamblage: Dexter Callender, Augusto Gandia, Aileen Iverson-Radtke

Carbon House (ARPA-E, 2020-2024)

Cinematic Details (Carbon House, 2022)


This investigation explores a design method for composite details. Through interdisciplinary work with a product designer, integral designs are developed beyond performance and optimization.
